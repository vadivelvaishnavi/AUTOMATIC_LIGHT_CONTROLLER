System Operation:  
The prototype successfully detects motion/presence using the IR sensor and switches the AC bulb ON via relay. When no motion is detected or ambient light is sufficient (with LDR integration), the system keeps the bulb OFF.

Performance & Reliability:
Relay switching is stable and responsive under varying conditions.
LED indicators provide clear feedback for power and light status.
The circuit operates consistently without microcontrollers, relying purely on analog components.

Energy Efficiency:
Prevents unnecessary lighting during daylight or idle periods.
Demonstrated potential to reduce electricity consumption by ~30% in test environments.

Educational & Practical Value:
Offers hands-on learning of sensor interfacing, comparator logic, and relay control.
Easy to simulate in Proteus/Multisim before PCB fabrication.
Suitable for DIY, academic projects, and low-cost automation.

Assistive Applications:
Enhances convenience for elderly or disabled individuals by eliminating manual switches.
Provides safe night-time navigation in hallways, bathrooms, and bedrooms.

Scalability & Deployment:
Can be extended to multiple rooms/zones with circuit duplication.
Works effectively in corridors, staircases, and outdoor spaces.

Limitations Observed:
Requires manual calibration of comparator thresholds.
No programmability for advanced features like timers or remote control.
Component aging (LDRs, relays) may affect long-term reliability.
Bulky when scaled across multiple rooms.
